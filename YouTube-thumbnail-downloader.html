Understanding Thumbnail Resolutions

YouTube generates multiple versions of a thumbnail. Our tool gives you access to all of them:

  • Max Resolution (4K/HD): 1920 x 1080 pixels. This is the best quality, but it's only available if the uploader uploaded a high-res image.
  • Standard Definition (SD): 640 x 480 pixels. Good for blog posts or small screens.
  • High Quality (HQ): 480 x 360 pixels. The standard image used by YouTube on mobile apps.
  • Medium Quality (MQ): 320 x 180 pixels. Suitable for very small previews.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is it legal to download thumbnails?

Yes, downloading thumbnails for personal use, fair use (like commentary, news reporting), or for your own videos is generally considered legal. However, you should not use someone else's copyrighted thumbnail for commercial purposes without permission.

Why can't I see the 4K option for some videos?

The 4K (MaxRes) option is only available if the original video uploader uploaded a high-quality image. If the video is very old or low quality, YouTube may only have HQ or SD versions available.

Does this work for YouTube Shorts?

Absolutely! Just copy the link of the Short (e.g., youtube.com/shorts/xyz...) and paste it above. It works exactly the same way.
